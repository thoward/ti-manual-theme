Introduction

The introduction of the electronic desktop calculator has revolutionized the business world. Many techniques that previously required large volumes of tables, tedious calculations, or access to larger computer centers can now be carried out in a fraction of the time. As modern businesses continue to grow and expand, however, so does the need for more advanced calculators capable of handling increasingly complicated calculations.

The TI-5310 Business Manager™ is designed to meet the needs of the modern business person. Many of the financial calculations required for today's business applications are too complex to be performed quickly and easily on standard desktop calculators. However, the specialized financial keys of the TI-5310 give you access to "built-in" financial functions that perform a complicated series of mathematical operations with only a few simple keystrokes.

For instance, suppose you have to decide whether to invest in a savings account or a capital asset. If you choose the capital asset, should you lease or buy the product? Although these may appear to be simple decisions at first glance, a number of complex calculations are involved. With the special features of the TI-5310, you can easily examine each alternative and select the one most profitable for you and your company.

Combining these financial keys with the best features available on most standard business machines, the TI-5310 gives you the speed and accuracy necessary to make better, more informed financial decisions.

Features

Financial Keys—Provide access to the calculator's built-in financial functions. Complicated calculations involving the time value of money can be solved with a few simple keystrokes.

Margin Mode—Complements the financial keys by allowing you to perform gross profit margin calculations involving an item's cost, selling price, and profit margin.

Independent Add Register—Performs addition and substraction with the familiar entry sequence of standard business machines. Multiplication and division are performed independently of any add/subtract operations in progress.

Full Memory—Provides four memory operations: sum to, subtract from, subtotal, or total the memory contents.

Keyboard—Consists of 38 touch keys including large total, subtotal, plus, minus, zero, and clear entry keys conveniently arranged on full-sized "Touch Operator" keyboard.

Impact Printer—Prints up to 12 digits, convenient comma punctuation, and an audit trail. The printer uses a standard 2-1/4-inch roll of paper and may be turned on or off with a special keyboard switch.

Ten-level Keyboard Buffering—Allows you to enter up to 10 additional digits and functions while the printer is operating.

Two-Key Rollover—Increases entry speed by allowing a second key to be pressed before the previously pressed key is released.

Decimal Switch—Displays and prints results in either floating or fixed-decimal (0, 2, 3, or 4 decimal places) mode. The add mode allows you to enter a series of numbers containing two decimal places, such as dollars and cents, without having to enter the decimal point.

Sigma Mode—Keeps a running total of all multiplication and division calculations by automatically summing the results to the independent add register.

Automatic Constant—Performs multiplication or division by a constant without reentry of the constant.

Percent Key—Calculates percentages, add-ons, and discounts.

5/4 Rounding Switch—Rounds the results of all multiplication and division calculations to the selected number of decimal places.

Non-Add Key for Dating and Numbering—Labels a tape with a date or stock number without affecting operations in progress.
